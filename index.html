Multimodal Biometrics for User Authentication on Mobile Devices

Advisor: Mikhail Gofman, PhD

Generally, modern mobile devices identify users based on a single biometric such as face, fingerprint or voice. Prior works have shown that a greater identification accuracy can be obtained by implementing feature-level fusion on multiple biometrics. We propose a novel feature-level fusion approach for combining histogram-oriented gradients features from the face with mel-frequency cepstral coefficient features from the voice. The classifiers we consider are KNN, LDA, QDA, RF and SVM. We find that for all classifiers, the multimodal approach significantly outperforms the unimodal face and voice approaches while taking less than 0.02 seconds to identify a user on a Samsung Galaxy S7 phone.

Predicting the Fragility of a Country

Advisor: Sherri Rose, PhD

Fragile countries are considered to be countries with unstable governments and politics along with limited resources. Identifying fragile countries may allow government officials and related personnel to anticipate changes in needed aid to minimize the severity of the effects from natural disasters and disease outbreaks. Using socio-economic data, we implement classifiers to predict fragile countries. Classifiers we considered were GLM, GLMNET, GAM, Classification Tree and Pruned Classification Tree. We have that a classification tree obtains the highest accuracy (90%) and a respectable R2 and AUC, while utilizing only two out of nine of our predictor variables, which is beneficial due to the difficulty of obtaining data from fragile countries.

Assessing Spatial and Temporal Variances of the Toxicity Levels of the Southern California Bight with Linear Mixed Models

Advisor: Laura Smith, PhD

The Southern California Coastal Water Research Project (SCCWRP) implements a regional monitoring program for the Southern California Bight (SCB). Data collected from sampled points of interest are used by SCCWRP to assess the toxicity levels of the SCB. In the past, SCCWRP used an equal number of new and previously visited sites for sampling. The decision of the ratio of new sites versus previously visited sites is of importance to SCCWRP. Using previously collected data, we use mixed-effect models to determine spatial and temporal variances of toxicity and abundance of metals. We have that the spatial variances exceeds the temporal variances for both variables. We conclude that the ratio of new sites and previously visited sites be adjusted in favor of more new sites for a better assessment of the SCB.

Risk Parity Control Portfolio Optimization with l 1 Regularization using Split Bregman

Advisor: Laura Smith, PhD

Often, portfolio optimization methods result in a portfolio divided among many assets, which is not ideal for transaction costs. Therefore, some existing methods use the l0 norm in the objective function to promote sparse solutions. Using the l0 norm results in a nonconvex objective function, often requiring complex methods to solve. We adapt the objective function from Feng and Palomar to include an l1 norm and use Split Bregman method to obtain the optimal and spare solution. We find that our proposed model produces sparse results with a lower volatility in comparison to various methods including the Feng-Palomar model while obtaining a significantly faster run-time than the Feng-Palomar model, especially when consider a large number of assets.
